The ruling BJP government suffered a stroke in their heartland yesterday by losing Gorakhpur, Araria and Phulpur Lok Sabha by-elections. With this loss and their earlier loss in Rajasthan by-polls and not so great performance in Gujarat, the probability of them coming back to power has reduced significantly. The field is wide open and the opposition will give BJP a run for their money. Should investors be worried about this? 

Before finding the answer to this question, let me take you back in time and show you the analysis of Sensex movement during the run up to elections in 1999, 2004, 2009 & 2014 as well post elections. 

## Summary 

Markets generally starts factoring in the uncertainty around election results six months before the polling dates and the policies of the new government gets factored in the first six months of the new government. This 12 month period is analysed and the results are tabulated below:

| Year | Outcome | Sensex 6 Month Before | Sensex 6 months after | Returns % |
|------|---------|-----------------------|----------------------|------------|
| 1999 | BJP  | 3686  | 5001  | 35% |
| 2004 | CONG | 5097  | 5704  | 12% |
| 2009 | CONG | 10631 | 15404 | 45% |
| 2014 | BJP  | 21196 | 27860 | 32% |

## Conclusion

As you can observe from the charts as well as the table, market had delivered positive returns during this regime change “interregnum” period. We expect the same trend to continue during 2019 election as well. Revival of Corporate Earnings, Rural Spending Boost, Return of Global Growth adds credence to the above thesis. 

Investors need not panic about the electoral outcome. We are 2.5 trillion Dollar economy. IMF, WB and other agencies expect us to grow @ 7% over the next decade and become a 5 trillion-dollar economy. Inflation rate at 5% will enable
the normal GDP to grow @ 12%. Sensex mirrors the growth of nominal GDP and expected to compound @ 12% over the next decade and touch six figure mark next decade. As long as we have favourable Demographics which is likely to last till 2045, our economy and markets will continue to grow independent of who
rules us. 

Sensex had delivered a 340 x return from 100 to 34000 since 1978, despite so many elections and regime changes. In the run up to elections, there will be periods of volatility and corrections, staying invested and in fact investing more
during this uncertain phase will be beneficial in the longer run as evident from past data.
